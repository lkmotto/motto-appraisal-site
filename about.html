The Appraiser's Perspective

Why an appraiser matters more than ever.

Everyone has a home value estimate now. Zillow, Redfin, Realtor.com — they all give you a number. And that number is wrong more often than people think. Zillow's own data shows a median 7.5% error on off-market homes. On a $400K DFW home, that's $30,000.

AVMs (Automated Valuation Models) work on averages. They pull comps from databases and run regression models. They're useful as a starting point. But they can't walk through your front door.

An appraiser sees the $40K kitchen renovation. The foundation crack under the carpet. The fact that the house backs to a commercial property. The AVM sees 2,100 square feet, 3 bed, 2 bath and calls it a number. I see the full picture.

The appraisal industry is under pressure from automated tools. But the value of a human who knows the market — who has walked thousands of properties in the same zip codes, who understands what makes one street worth 15% more than the next — that's not something you can automate.

I'm not anti-technology. I use data every day. But data without context is just numbers. My job is to add the context.

That's the appraisal perspective no algorithm can give you.
