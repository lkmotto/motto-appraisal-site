Roanoke's Real Estate Market: What Makes It Unique

Roanoke's market has several distinctive characteristics that affect how appraisals are performed and how values should be interpreted:

  • TX-114/US-377 growth corridor: New commercial development along both highways has increased land values and created demand for residential properties with highway proximity — but has also created noise and traffic considerations that appraisers must account for.
  • Denton-Tarrant county line: The 76262 zip code spans both counties. Properties in Denton County and Tarrant County portions of Roanoke pull from different comp pools and may have different tax structures. An appraiser who regularly works both counties — as Luke Motto does — handles this split correctly.
  • Mix of old and new construction: Roanoke has a wide range of property vintages, from established mid-century homes near downtown to brand-new construction in master-planned communities. These properties do not appraise the same way, and using the wrong comparable sales produces flawed values.
  • Proximity to Westlake and Trophy Club: High-value adjacent communities create upward price pressure on Roanoke properties with good access and school district assignment, but the premium must be calibrated correctly.
  • Northwest ISD and Keller ISD: School district assignment affects buyer demand and property values in this area. Many Roanoke properties fall within Northwest ISD, while some areas pull from Keller ISD. This distinction matters for comp selection.
